Blackhawk & Uber Rideshare Partnership

Intro & Why: The new ride share partnership with Uber

BTC, in an effort to improve services and strengthen the community, proudly presents the new "BTC & Uber ride service" and "BTC & Uber App". With the new ride share service, students can hail a 'BTC-Uber' driver and travel to a campus or nearby place for free.

The App

The convenient "BTC & Uber" app can be used on any campus.

Main App Features

To use the app, simply start the program, sign in, and press the big red "hail" button. After pressing the button, you'll be given in-app notifications on the important stuff. Notifications include driver location, estimated time of arrival, and drivers that just entered your hailing range.

Other Usefull features

The app is not limited to just a big red "hail" button and notifications. For any given reason at any given time, you may choose from the adjacent list of other drivers near your hailing range. Selecting from the list also allows you to select your destination ahead of time via embedded Google maps service.

Setting Up The "BTC & Uber App"

Installation is simple, just search for "BTC & Uber App" on Google Play (Android/OS), Apple's app store (I/OS), or Windows Marketplace (Windows smartphones). Head to the app's in-store page and press download. After installing the software, sign-up in any "sign-up" page by using your BTC login information. Proceed by opening the "BTC & Uber App", and press the "link-me" button. A prompt will show asking for your student ID. Enter your ID and press "ok" and you're done. After completing the process, know that the app and your BTC account are linked, and you'll never have to link your app to the account again under normal circumstances.

F.A.Q.

Can there be a shortage of drivers?

BTC does it's best to ensure all of it's services are running when they should. To do this, BTC may hire Uber drivers outside the BTC community. However, despite all the hard work and logistics done by BTC staff, such a driver shortage is still posable, but highly unlikely.

Who selects drivers?

BTC would normally reach out to the BTC students & staff to enhance the sense of community. However, to meet current and posable demands we may borrow from Uber's pool of preselected drivers. Drivers from Uber, though, are temporary. Whenever posable, non-BTC Uber drivers are dropped for available BTC-Uber drivers.

How are drivers approved?

Drivers must meet criteria and tests from both Uber and goverment legal requirements. BTC also has it's own criteria drivers must meet, and are tailored for student safety.

How do I (a BTC student or staff) become a driver?

Simply go to the welcome center and they'll explain everything including how one goes about the tests. If you manage to pass all the criteria alive, then you can be a BTC Uber driver.

Can I be revoked from being a BTC Uber driver?

Yes, BTC reserves the right to do so for any given reason, but usually, it's because a law is broken, a rule is broken, the driver in question has decided to quit, or is dead. If you were revoked for voluntarily quitting, then you can become a BTC-Uber driver again by repassing all criteria, though, this isn't an option if you've been revoked for reasons involving student safety or the above.
